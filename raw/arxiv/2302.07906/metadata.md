## Key extracted claims

- GRB 221009A is described as the brightest burst ever observed in this source framing.
- The source uses Eiso ≈ 10^55 erg and z ≈ 0.15 as context for the event's extremeness.
- The paper presents multiwavelength observations covering the first three months of afterglow evolution.
- The X-ray brightness decays as a power law with slope approximately t^-1.66.
- The authors state that this X-ray behavior is not consistent with standard predictions for jetted emission.
- The authors attribute the behavior to a shallow energy profile of the relativistic jet.
- The paper argues that a similar trend in other energetic GRBs suggests the most extreme explosions may be powered by structured jets launched by a common central engine.

## Caveats

- The measured long-lived X-ray decay and multiwavelength coverage are observational constraints; the shallow structured jet and common-engine interpretation are model interpretations.
- This source should be compared against LHAASO's narrow jet / structured core interpretation and Laskar et al.'s radio/mm extra component, not merged into a single agreed model without qualification.
