# Properties of the Extremely Energetic GRB 221009A from Konus-Wind and SRG/ART-XC Observations

## Key extracted claims

- Konus-Wind and Mikhail Pavlinsky ART-XC observed GRB 221009A.
- The paper describes GRB 221009A as the brightest gamma-ray burst detected by Konus-Wind in more than 28 years of observations.
- The pulsed prompt phase lasted about 600 s.
- A steady power-law decay followed and lasted more than 25 ks.
- The time-averaged spectral peak energy was Ep ≈ 2.6 MeV.
- Ep at the brightest emission peak was ≈ 3.0 MeV.
- The total 20 keV–10 MeV energy fluence was ≈ 0.22 erg cm^-2.
- The peak energy flux in the same band was ≈ 0.031 erg cm^-2 s^-1.
- At z = 0.151, the paper derives Eiso ≈ 1.2×10^55 erg and Liso ≈ 3.4×10^54 erg s^-1 on a 64 ms scale.
- The authors state that the isotropic energetics fit both Amati and Yonetoku hardness–intensity correlations for more than 300 Konus-Wind long GRBs.

## Caveats

- Eiso and Liso are isotropic-equivalent values and depend on the adopted redshift and measurement band.
- “Very hard, super-energetic version of a normal long GRB” is the authors' interpretation, not a direct observation fact.
- ART-XC observed the event outside its field of view through the telescope side structure; the paper treats ART-XC primarily as a robust light-curve shape constraint rather than direct on-axis spectroscopy.
