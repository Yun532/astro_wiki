## Key extracted claims

- The paper studies GRB 221009A with INTEGRAL/IBIS-PICsIT spectral-timing data.
- PICsIT covers 200–2600 keV for the analysis and provides 7.8 ms time resolution in broad energy bands.
- INTEGRAL was observing XTE J1701-462 at the GRB trigger time, with GRB 221009A about 65.8° off-axis from the pointing direction.
- Prompt emission lasted more than 600 s, including the precursor.
- PICsIT found flux-tracking spectral behavior: the spectrum is softer when brighter.
- The spectral-index / flux relation changes during the burst and differs across pulses and rise/decay phases.
- PICsIT afterglow emission begins to dominate at about T0 + 630 s.
- The afterglow decay slope is 1.6 ± 0.2, consistent with reported soft-X-ray slopes.
- The authors removed bad time intervals with three or more missing bins and report no time bins near the maximum telemetry value, indicating PICsIT did not suffer pile-up effects during the burst.

## Caveats

- PICsIT had telemetry gaps during parts of the brightest prompt pulses, so peak behavior in some intervals is not fully observed.
- The flux-tracking and changing spectral-index / flux relation are spectral-evolution results; proposed physical emission-process interpretations should be attributed to the authors.
