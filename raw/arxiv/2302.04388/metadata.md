## Key extracted claims

- The paper presents multiwavelength observations spanning 15 orders of magnitude in photon energy from radio to gamma rays.
- The observational campaign extends to about 100 days after discovery in the paper's framing.
- The data can be partially explained by a forward shock from a highly collimated relativistic jet interacting with a low-density wind-like medium.
- Under that model, the beaming-corrected kinetic energy is EK ~ 4×10^50 erg, typical for the GRB population.
- Radio and mm data strongly constrain the forward-shock model but require an additional emission component.
- Equipartition arguments suggest the radio emission is produced by a small amount of mass, <=6×10^-7 solar masses, moving relativistically with Gamma >= 9 and kinetic energy >=10^49 erg.
- The temporal evolution of this component does not follow standard single-power-law synchrotron prescriptions such as reverse shock or two-component jet, nor a thermal electron population.
- The authors state GRB 221009A may remain detectable with radio telescopes for years.

## Caveats

- Forward shock, wind-like medium, additional radio component, equipartition mass, Lorentz factor and kinetic energy are model interpretations, not direct observation facts.
- The paper explicitly notes the standard model does not explain all data, especially radio/mm behavior.
